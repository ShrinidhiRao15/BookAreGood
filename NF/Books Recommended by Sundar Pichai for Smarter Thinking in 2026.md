# 📚 6 Must‑Read Books Recommended by Sundar Pichai for Smarter Thinking in 2026

Google CEO **Sundar Pichai** has curated a diverse reading list for 2026 that goes beyond technology and business. His recommendations focus on developing **better thinking frameworks, resilience, innovation, and leadership**, drawing lessons from science, history, memoirs, and entrepreneurship.

---

## 1. **The Gene: An Intimate History**  
**Author:** Siddhartha Mukherjee  

This book explores the history and science of genetics, tracing humanity’s understanding of heredity from ancient ideas to modern DNA research. It blends scientific discovery with ethical questions, helping readers think critically about how technology shapes human life.  
**Why it matters:** Encourages scientific literacy and ethical reasoning. [3](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/life-style/books/features/6-must-read-books-recommended-by-sundar-pichai-for-smarter-thinking-in-2026/articleshow/130733481.cms)

---

## 2. **A Beautiful Mind**  
**Author:** Sylvia Nasar  

A biography of Nobel Prize–winning mathematician **John Nash**, this book combines intellectual brilliance with a deeply human story of living with schizophrenia.  
**Why it matters:** Highlights perseverance, creativity, and the complexity of human intelligence. [3](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/life-style/books/features/6-must-read-books-recommended-by-sundar-pichai-for-smarter-thinking-in-2026/articleshow/130733481.cms)

---

## 3. **The Wright Brothers**  
**Author:** David McCullough  

This historical account tells the story of Wilbur and Orville Wright and their relentless pursuit of human flight. It focuses on patience, experimentation, and disciplined problem‑solving.  
**Why it matters:** Demonstrates how breakthrough innovation comes from curiosity and persistence. [3](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/life-style/books/features/6-must-read-books-recommended-by-sundar-pichai-for-smarter-thinking-in-2026/articleshow/130733481.cms)

---

## 4. **Born a Crime**  
**Author:** Trevor Noah  

A memoir about growing up in apartheid‑era South Africa, blending humor with serious reflections on race, identity, and resilience.  
**Why it matters:** Builds empathy and shows how adversity shapes perspective and leadership. [3](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/life-style/books/features/6-must-read-books-recommended-by-sundar-pichai-for-smarter-thinking-in-2026/articleshow/130733481.cms)

---

## 5. **The Lean Startup**  
**Author:** Eric Ries  

A modern entrepreneurship classic that introduces lean principles such as rapid experimentation, validated learning, and iterative development.  
**Why it matters:** Teaches adaptable thinking and efficient decision‑making in uncertain environments. [2](https://www.msn.com/en-in/news/other/sundar-pichai-s-2026-reading-list-blends-science-memoir-and-innovation/gm-GM3F6D7A49)

---

## 6. **Trillion Dollar Coach**  
**Authors:** Eric Schmidt, Jonathan Rosenberg, Alan Eagle  

The story of **Bill Campbell**, legendary coach to Silicon Valley leaders, emphasizing trust, empathy, and people‑first leadership.  
**Why it matters:** Shows that strong leadership is rooted in human connection, not just strategy. [2](https://www.msn.com/en-in/news/other/sundar-pichai-s-2026-reading-list-blends-science-memoir-and-innovation/gm-GM3F6D7A49)

---

## 🔍 Key Takeaway

Sundar Pichai’s 2026 reading list reinforces the idea that **smarter thinking comes from diverse perspectives**—combining science, human stories, disciplined innovation, and compassionate leadership. Together, these books help readers sharpen decision‑making, build resilience, and lead effectively in complex systems.
